Roti-Tarkari



Embark on a culinary journey with the authentic flavors of Roti-Tarkari, a classic Nepali dish that harmonizes simplicity with rich taste. Soft, unleavened rotis, crafted from whole wheat flour, serve as the perfect vessel for an array of vibrant vegetable curries (Tarkari). Each bite is a delightful amalgamation of textures and tastes, where the wholesome roti complements the aromatic and savory Tarkari. Whether enjoyed as a comforting homely meal or a satisfying street food experience, Roti-Tarkari captures the essence of Nepal's culinary heritage.

Chana-Masala



Delight in the aromatic allure of Nepali Chana Masala, a flavorful dish that showcases the versatility of chickpeas. Simmered in a fragrant blend of spices, tomatoes, and onions, Chana Masala offers a hearty and satisfying meal. Whether enjoyed with rice or accompanied by flatbreads, this dish is a celebration of the rich and diverse flavors found in Nepali cuisine.


Mo:Mo


Experience the culinary artistry of Nepali Momos, delectable dumplings that have captured hearts worldwide. These delicate parcels are filled with a flavorful mixture of minced meat or vegetables, encased in a thin layer of dough and steamed or fried to perfection. Served with a tangy dipping sauce, Momos are a symbol of Nepali hospitality and a must-try for those seeking a taste of Himalayan comfort and flavor.


Jeri


Transport yourself to a realm of sweetness with Nepali Jeri, a traditional sweet treat that captivates with its delicate and syrupy goodness. Made by deep-frying pretzel-shaped dough and then soaking it in sugar syrup, Jeri offers a delightful balance of crispy texture and sugary indulgence. Perfect for festive occasions or as a sweet pick-me-up, Jeri is a cherished part of Nepali dessert culture.


Chatpate


Indulge your taste buds with the bold and vibrant flavors of Nepali Chatpate. A popular street snack, Chatpate combines puffed rice, roasted peanuts, fried lentils, and a medley of chopped vegetables, creating a symphony of textures and tastes. Seasoned with a spicy and tangy blend of spices, this savory delight is a go-to treat for those seeking a burst of flavor and crunch in every bite.

Chhaang


Discover the traditional flavors of Nepal with Chhaang, a unique and culturally significant beverage. Made from fermented millet or barley, Chhaang has been a part of Nepali festivities and gatherings for centuries. Its mildly alcoholic content adds a touch of warmth to social occasions. Enjoyed during festivals and communal celebrations, Chhaang reflects the deep-rooted traditions and convivial spirit of Nepali culture. Experience the authenticity and heritage of Nepal through this time-honored beverage.


Lassi


Indulge in the refreshing allure of Nepali Lassi, a timeless yogurt-based beverage that captivates the senses. Crafted with skillful precision, this velvety concoction strikes a perfect balance between the creaminess of yogurt and the subtle sweetness of sugar. Whether enjoyed in its classic form or infused with vibrant flavors like mango or rose, Nepali Lassi is a cooling elixir that provides respite on warm days. Served chilled and often garnished with a sprinkle of ground spices, every sip of Lassi is a harmonious blend of tradition and culinary innovation, making it a beloved companion to Nepali meals or a standalone treat to beat the heat.

Special

Here are List of Our Special Foods that we offer here:

Dhido


Dhido is a traditional Nepali staple, cherished for its simplicity and cultural significance. Crafted from buckwheat or millet flour and water, the mixture is meticulously stirred during cooking, resulting in a dense, sticky, and elastic mass. Often served with an array of side dishes such as pickles, vegetables, and meat, Dhido stands as a symbol of Nepalese culinary heritage. Its preparation and consumption are deeply woven into the fabric of Gurung and Magar communities, making it a cherished part of local traditions.


juju-Dhau


Indulge in the exquisite taste of Juju Dhau, affectionately known as "king curd." Hailing from Bhaktapur in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal, this special yogurt is celebrated for its creamy texture and delightful sweetness. Served in charming clay pots, Juju Dhau takes center stage during festivals and special occasions. A symbol of culinary craftsmanship, this traditional delight is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of Nepal.


Sukuti(Dry Meat)


Savor the robust flavors of Sukuti(Dry Meat), a beloved Nepali treat that transcends time and tradition. Crafted from buffalo or goat meat, Sukuti undergoes a meticulous marination process with a medley of spices before being air-dried or sun-dried. The result is a delectable dried meat that serves as a popular snack or accompaniment. Enjoy Sukuti with rice or alongside your favorite beverage, reveling in the distinctive taste that embodies the essence of Nepali cuisine. A common delight in mountainous regions, Sukuti is a flavorful journey through Nepal's culinary landscape.
